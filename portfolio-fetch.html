Fetch!

The Process

#1. Overview

Fetch! was developed by my partner and I. This was our first pair-programmed project. Throughout the project, I played the role of navigator and my partner was the driver. In the beginning, we agreed upon whose responsibility it was to style the app - my partner - and who would do all the necessary research - myself. To develop Fetch! we used the MEN Stack and Places API by Google. This was our first time working with an API - before fully grasping what they were. The Places API by Google is extensive and detailed. Therefore, it was my role to read through the documentation and understand how to utilize it properly. At first, it was challenging to learn how to efficiently query the database to receive results that were only dog-friendly locations in Chicago, since there are various ways to achieve the desired outcome.Once we got passed those hurdles the result ended up being a dog-friendly centric app.

#2. Context & Challenge

Like the other projects, this too was built in a five-day sprint. This project was nearly completely pair-programmed. I played the role of the driver and my partner the navigator. As a pair, we worked really well together. We both were flexible with one another and supported each other throughout the process. We were in constant communication with one another and we did not proceed without making the other aware of our intentions or approval. The rare moments we did work independently, we had agreed upon beforehand. We are both dog lovers and enjoy taking our dogs with us. We wanted to create an app that centered around the dog lover community. We found it hard to find information on dog-friendly establishments and any unique places that dog parents love to take them. Therefore, our goal was to create an app that would resolve both of those problems. We extracted dog-friendly establishments from the Places API and took in places that were user recommended. As a team, we would set daily goals and the desired timeframe to have them accomplished by. For example, the first day we were determined to have our server and MongoDB setup, finish the authentication routes, and if we had time set up our landing page.
