The Process

#1. Overview

Battleship is a classic game, everyone knows and (probably) has played in their lifetime. Two players place their fleet of five ships on a grid, then take turns calling out coordinates, responding with either there was hit or miss, and the player who sinks their opponent’s ship wins. Game-play, in person, is very intuitive and straight-forward. Recreating that type of game-play in the browser is not as intuitive and straight-forward as I had imagined it required a lot of game-logic and granular thought process.

I choose to create Battle-At-Sea because I love to see recreations and people’s personal take on classics. Therefore, I wanted to create my own version of Battleship. Playing different versions of Battleship online, I have yet to come across one that paid homage to retro video game styles.Therefore, my goals were not only to recreate game-play but to style it in a way that will give the players a sense of nostalgia. I wanted players to feel as if they were kids again at the arcade while playing my game - that was my number one priority.

#2. Process & Insight

Playing various versions of Battleship, I had always liked the two grid setup. I appreciated the very minimalistic feel of Pac-man and Space Invader these games inspired the look of my game. I had taken a day to style the site and rest was creating the game logic. It was challenging at first to understand how the game will:

  • How to place the ships
  • Determine whose board and what coordinate was clicked
  • Who made the click
  • If that click resulted in a hit or miss
  • If it was a hit are all the ships sunk or not
  • If not or if it was miss switch players
